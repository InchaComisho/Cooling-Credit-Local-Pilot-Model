# Minimum MRV Protocol

## Minimum Measurement, Reporting, and Verification Procedure for Small-Scale Cooling Credit Pilots

[日本語](MINIMUM_MRV_PROTOCOL_ja.md) | [English](MINIMUM_MRV_PROTOCOL.md) | [العربية](MINIMUM_MRV_PROTOCOL_ar.md)

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the minimum MRV procedure for starting small-scale Cooling Credit pilots in schools, shopping streets, parks, farms, shelters, bus stops, public facilities, and other measurable local heat-stress sites.

MRV means:

```text
Measurement
Reporting
Verification
```

This protocol is not an official international standard or public certification system. It is a practical minimum procedure for starting local pilots.

---

## 2. Minimum Measurement Items

For the first local pilot, the following items should be measured at minimum:

```text
Air temperature
Humidity
WBGT
Surface temperature
Before-and-after photos
```

If possible, also record:

```text
Thermal images
Location of measurement points
Time of measurement
Weather
Solar exposure
Wind condition
```

---

## 3. Recommended Measurement Items

To improve reliability, the following indicators are recommended when available:

```text
Soil moisture
Water use
Rainwater use
Electricity use
Greening area
Shade area
Compost or humus volume
Food-waste reduction
Outdoor AC exhaust temperature
Pedestrian or user comfort and stayability
```

These indicators help evaluate not only cooling effects, but also water-cycle recovery, soil restoration, waste-heat mitigation, and local use value.

---

## 4. Measurement Timing

At minimum, measure twice:

```text
Before implementation
After implementation
```

A better measurement schedule is:

```text
Before implementation: multiple measurements at the same time of day
Immediately after implementation: implementation day
Post-implementation: after one week, one month, and during summer peak
Continuous monitoring: weekly or monthly
```

For comparison, measurements should be taken at the same time, at the same location, and under similar weather conditions whenever possible.

---

## 5. Measurement Points

At least one measurement point should be placed inside the pilot area. If possible, define one comparison point.

```text
Intervention point: where the cooling action is implemented
Comparison point: a nearby place without the cooling action
```

Examples:

```text
water-retentive schoolyard area vs ordinary asphalt surface
mist-cooled shopping street point vs non-mist point
greened flower bed area vs bare ground or pavement
outdoor AC waste-heat mitigation point vs non-mitigated point
```

## 7. Photos and Thermal Images

Photos should be taken from the same angle before and after implementation.

```text
before photo
after photo
measurement-device display photo
surface photo
thermal image
surrounding-context photo
```

Photos alone should not be used to claim results. They should support numerical measurements as evidence.

---

## 8. Safety Considerations

When using mist cooling or water-based interventions, check the following:

```text
whether WBGT worsens under high humidity
whether the surface becomes slippery
whether water contacts electrical equipment
whether water use is excessive
whether hygiene or mold risks emerge
whether pedestrian or evacuation routes are blocked
whether heatstroke prevention is prioritized
```

Mist cooling must be evaluated using not only air temperature, but also humidity and WBGT.

---

## 9. Evaluation Policy

Small-scale pilots do not need to become formal credits at the first stage.

A provisional evaluation can be used:

```text
No measurement: reference activity
Measurement exists: cooling activity candidate
Before-and-after comparison exists: Local Cooling Point candidate
Continuous measurement exists: Local Cooling Point
Public data exists: Cooling Credit pilot candidate
```

---

## 10. Conclusion

The first step of a Cooling Credit local pilot is not to create a perfect certification system.

The first step is to measure locally, implement a cooling action, compare before and after, and preserve evidence.

```text
Measure
↓
Cool
↓
Record
↓
Compare
↓
Disclose
```

This minimum MRV procedure becomes the foundation for Local Cooling Points and future Cooling Credit institutionalization.
